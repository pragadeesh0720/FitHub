Triceps Cable Workouts



Cable Push Down

The cable tricep pushdown is an exercise that primarily targets the triceps muscles of the arms.




1.You can use any attachment for this. The cable should be set all the way at the top of the machine.

2.Make sure to keep your upper arm glued at your side. Extend your elbows until you feel your triceps contract.

Cable Cross Pushdown

The cable cross pushdown is a variation of the tricep pushdown exercise that targets the lateral head of the triceps.




1.Either use two handles or no attachment at all. Both sides of the cable crossover should be set to the top.

2.Stand in the center of the machine holding each Cable in the opposing hand. Your upper arm should be at a 45 degree angle with your torso.

3.Extend your elbows until you feel a contraction and then return to the starting position

Cable Skullcrusher

The cable skullcrusher is a variation of the traditional skullcrusher exercise that targets the triceps muscles of the arms.




1.You can use a straight bar, rope, easy bar, or two handles. The cable should be set all the way to the top of the machine.

2.Face away from the machine with your elbows up and the attachment just behind your head.

3.Extend at the elbows until you feel a contraction in your triceps and then flex at the elbow back to the starting position.

Cable Overhead Tricep Extension


he cable overhead tricep extension is an exercise that targets the long head of the triceps muscles of the arms.




1.You can use any attachment. The cable should be all the way to the bottom of the machine. Face away from the machine.

2.Point your elbows straight up toward the ceiling.

3.From there, extend your elbows until your fists are pointed straight toward the ceiling.
