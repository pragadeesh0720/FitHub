CHEST WORKOUTS


PUSH UP

"Pushups are a popular exercise that primarily targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps while also engaging the core and lower body."




1.Place your hands firmly on the ground, directly under shoulders.

2.Flatten your back so your entire body is straight and slowly lower your body

3.Draw shoulder blades back and down, keeping elbows tucked close to your body

4.Exhale as you push back to the starting position



INCLINE PUSH UP

An incline pushup is a variation of the traditional pushup where the hands are placed on an elevated surface, making the exercise easier and targeting the upper chest muscles.




1.Stand facing bench or sturdy elevated platform

2.Place hands on edge of bench or platform, slightly wider than shoulder width.

3.Slowly lower your body until your chest almost touches the bench

4.Push body up until arms are extended.

DECLINE PUSH UP

Decline pushup is a challenging variation of the standard pushup that involves elevating the feet to increase the difficulty and engage the upper chest muscles.




1.Use a bench to elevate your feet.

2.Put your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width.

3.Slowly lower your body until your chest almost touches the ground

4.Raise your body until you almost lock your elbows.



DIPS

The dip is a compound, bodyweight exercise. You grab two parallel bars, hoist yourself up, and then lower your entire body by bending your elbows.
When you hear “ compound exercise ,” it’s referring to multi-joint movements that work several muscles or muscle groups at the same time.



1.Hold your body with arms locked above the equipment

2.Lower your body slowly while leaning forward, flare out your elbows

3.Raise your body above the bars until your arms are lock
